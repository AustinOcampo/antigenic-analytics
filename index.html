The Blind Spots

Three gaps your current stack can't close

Most fraud tools are built for speed — catching known schemes in real time. But behavioral fraud operates on a different timeline entirely.

RULES

Rules catch what you already know

Your rules engine detects the patterns you've already coded for. It can't surface the schemes you haven't seen yet — and fraudsters adapt faster than rule libraries update.

MODEL

ML returns a score, not an answer

When your Head of Risk asks why an account was flagged, "the model said so" isn't defensible. Black-box scores create investigation bottlenecks, not clarity.

TIME

Nobody's watching the slow game

Behavioral fraud is patient — staying below thresholds, rotating identities, spacing transactions over months. Point-in-time checks will never catch a 12-month pattern.

-- Signal: Loyalty redemption velocity spike
-- Flags accounts with sudden bursts of
-- redemption activity vs. their baseline

WITH baseline AS (
  SELECT account_id,
    AVG(monthly_redemptions) AS avg_rate,
    STDDEV(monthly_redemptions) AS std_rate
  FROM redemption_monthly_agg
  WHERE period >= DATEADD('month', -12, CURRENT_DATE)
  GROUP BY account_id
)
SELECT r.account_id,
  r.current_month_redemptions,
  b.avg_rate,
  ROUND((r.current_month_redemptions - b.avg_rate)
    / NULLIF(b.std_rate, 0), 2) AS z_score
FROM current_redemptions r
JOIN baseline b ON r.account_id = b.account_id
WHERE z_score > 3.0;
