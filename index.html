The Problem

The fraud costing you the most
is the kind built to evade you.

Sophisticated fraudsters don't trip your rules — they reverse-engineer them. They stay below thresholds, rotate patterns, and blend into normal behavior. Behavioral signals catch what rules and scores structurally can't.

01
Rule-based systems
Catch exactly what they were programmed for. Nothing more. Sophisticated fraud is engineered specifically to stay just below the thresholds you've set.
02
ML black boxes
Return a score, not an explanation. When your CFO asks why a vendor was flagged, "the model said so" isn't an answer you can act on or defend.
03
Point-in-time audits
Miss fraud designed to stay below monthly review thresholds. The pattern only surfaces across 6–12 months of behavioral data — not a snapshot.
→
What's actually needed
Explainable behavioral signals, computed from your own data, with verdicts clear enough to walk into any executive meeting and defend line by line.

I've spent my career inside the fraud teams of some of the largest companies in the world. The problem I kept running into wasn't a lack of tools — it was that every tool was optimized for the fraud it had already seen.

Antigenic Analytics is named after antigenic variation — the biological mechanism by which pathogens mutate their surface proteins to evade immune detection. Sophisticated fraudsters do the same: they study your detection systems and adapt specifically to stay below your thresholds.

My background in econometrics and psychology means every signal has a statistical foundation and a behavioral rationale — not just a threshold someone guessed at.

I built this open source because the best proof is letting you run it yourself. No demo calls. No pitch decks. The repo is the pitch.
